SENVA

Lead Developer
January 2022 - June 2022

Surface Exploration & Navigation Visual Assistant (SENVA) is an AR heads up display built on the HoloLens 2 platform for the NASA SUITS Challenge. SENVA is intended as a proof of concept to demonstrate expanded capabilities for next-gen spacesuits for lunar/martian exploration by lowering users' cognitive load and improving safety. SENVA is specifically designed to work in a high risk environment, employing multi-modal input for redundancy, as well as a completely off-cloud NLP powered digital assistant trained on Apollo mission transcripts to understand domain-specific language used in spaceflight operations. Additional safety features include proximity terrain analysis, dynamic pathfinding, search and rescue capabilities, and a blackbox feature for recording telemetry data.

Responsibilities

  • Led multiple interdisciplinary divisions from University of Southern California with a total of 60 members
  • Developed spatial mapping based terrain analysis and integrated all other features into a cohesive product
  • Set up a streamlined toolchain setup process to expedite development on the Hololens 2, a novel platform
  • Sourced Windows PCs from USC through Planet Save to enable macOS team members to develop on Hololens 2
