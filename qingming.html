Surface Exploration & Navigation Visual Assistant (SENVA) is a holographic heads up display built on the HoloLens 2 for the 2021 NASA SUITS Challenge. SENVA is intended as a proof of concept to demonstrate expanded capabilities for NASA's next-gen xEMU suit intended for lunar exploration. Features include proximity terrain analysis, dynamic pathfinding, realtime yellow brick road navigation, an offline NLP digital assistant powered by machine learning, and search and rescue capabilities, and comprehensive telemetry stream from suit sensors for vitals monitoring.

Responsibilities

  • Led multiple interdisciplinary divisions from University of Southern California with a total of 60 members
  • Developed spatial mapping based terrain analysis and integrated all other features into a cohesive product
  • Set up a streamlined toolchain setup process to expedite development on the Hololens 2, a novel platform
  • Sourced Windows PCs from USC through Planet Save to enable macOS team members to develop on Hololens 2
